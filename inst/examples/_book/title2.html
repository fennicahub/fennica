13.0.3 Top-20 title for “Language material” content type

In the Fennica dataset, language material makes up the majority of the content, comprising 91.6% of the entire dataset. This includes written texts, documents, books, articles, and any other content primarily composed of language. The remaining 8.4% consists of non-language material, such as maps, music, computer files, and other types of content that do not primarily rely on language. For a more detailed breakdown of the different types of content within the dataset, you can refer to chapter “Types of records” of this e-book.

13.2 Description

MARC21 field 008 contains fixed-length data elements that encode various bibliographic details. In this context, we focus specifically on books—that is, records where the Type of Record is language material. From this field, we extract and interpret the genre information as encoded in position 33 of the 008 field.

This analysis is based on r sum(nrow(df)) book records in the Fennica database.
